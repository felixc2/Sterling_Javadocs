InventoryStatusTransition Associated With Table: YFS_INVENTORY_STATUS_TRAN
To support automatic change of inventory status and therefore the supplies associated with it, zones have an inventory status transition matrix associated with them. What this implies is that as a result of an operation if inventory is moved to a specific zone, inventory status would automatically change and as a result the supply picture would change automatically. For example, let's consider Inventory in status Awaiting QC check in the QC area. Once the inventory check is done and the product moved to a zone where put away of QC okay products are typically stored, then the inventory status changes from Awaiting QC to Normal.

StorageConstraint Associated With Table: YFS_STORAGE_CONSTRAINT
This table stores one or more records indicating what items or category of items may be stored in a zone/location. This could also be imagined to as having dedication information for a zone/location for an item, product line, velocity, catalog organization, etc. While doing put away, these constraints are used to determine the zone to stow away received inventory. Zones or locations with no storage constraints can store any inventory.

Zone Associated With Table: YFS_ZONE
This table stores the zone level setup details for a warehouse. The warehouse may be divided into multiple zones based on the product storage, packaging types, etc. Zone is a way of classifying a set of locations that share common properties. Locations belong to zones. Zone level storage constraints are auto propagated to locations through membership in a zone. The mix and do not mix constraints at zone level, apply to every single location in the zone.

ZoneLocation Associated With Table: YFS_LOCATION
Warehouse zones are further divided into locations of different sizes where inventory is stored. Each location belongs to a zone and a node. It is classified as INTRANSIT, REGULAR, VIRTUAL, STAGING or DOCK. It has attributes like aisle number, bay number, level number etc.

VelocityCodestring (maximum length: 40) yfs_location.velocity_code
This field indicates that the items are classified as 'A', 'B' or 'C' class items based on whether they are fast selling, not so fast selling and low selling items. These item classifications are typically followed by all enterprises to optimize certain operations such as sourcing and stocking. The reason to have locations that prefer certain velocity codes is to have locations closer to dock stocking 'A' class items, and locations furthest away from the dock stocking 'C' class items. The velocity code is a preference on the location and not a constraint. If 'A' class items fill up all locations meant for 'A' class items, then they can be moved to 'B' and later 'C'. Similarly, 'C' can go to 'B' and 'A' for lack of space in the respective locations preferred for a specific velocity code. The 'B' class items move into 'C' and then into 'A'.
